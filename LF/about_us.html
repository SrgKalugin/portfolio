The History of Falafel

‘Tasty little thing’ is the literal translation of the word falafel. We think this fits like perfectly tailored gloves. In some other cultures, it also means ‘food’, capturing how food should look, feel and taste.

It is rumoured that over a millennia ago, some monks in Egypt discovered falafel, and they were so taken by it that consumption spread from Alexandria to Mesopotamia and beyond. Since the 1940s it has spread to UK kitchens and taste buds.

Falafel is what you make of it. Monks in Egypt still eat large amounts of it during religious ceremonies and celebrations. It is a healthy and filling street food in the Middle East. McDonald’s serve falafel as its vegetarian choice in many countries. It is almost the only choice of vegetarian/vegan lunch in the UK. It is a hipster food in Germany and France. Falafel even made an appearance in the popular TV show ‘Suits’ as a peace offering by high-flying corporate lawyers. Now, as a result of London Falafel, it is also an artisan food.
